Software and Hardware

POS

Hardware refers to the physical components of a computer, such as the CPU, memory, and peripherals. In contrast, software is the intangible set of instructions, programs, and data that directs the hardware to perform specific tasks.

Security

CCTV Installation

A security camera, also known as a surveillance camera or CCTV (Closed Circuit Television) camera, is a device designed to capture and record video footage for the purpose of monitoring and enhancing security.

Remote Monitoring

Remote monitoring refers to the process of observing, tracking, and managing the status and performance of systems, devices, or environments from a distant location. This practice leverages technology to gather data and provide insights, allowing users to make informed decisions without being physically present.

Software Development

Software development is the process of designing, creating, testing, and maintaining software applications, frameworks, or other software components. It involves a series of stages and methodologies to ensure that the software meets the needs of its users and performs as expected.

Network WiFi

Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity) is a technology that allows electronic devices to connect to a wireless local area network (WLAN), typically using the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz radio frequency bands. Wi-Fi networks provide internet access and enable communication between devices without the need for physical cables.

Biometric Time and Attendance

Biometric time and attendance systems use unique biological characteristics to identify and verify employees when they clock in and out of work. These systems ensure accurate time tracking, enhance security, and prevent time fraud (e.g., buddy punching, where one employee clocks in for another).

Laptops

Laptops are designed for mobile use, and they are powered by a rechargeable battery, although they can also be plugged into an electrical outlet. They usually come equipped with various input/output ports, a touchpad or trackpad for mouse functions, and often a webcam and microphone for video conferencing and other functions.
